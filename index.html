PTO & Sick Leave policy reference (Handbook §7.5)

Paid Time Off (PTO) — by tenure

The tracker uses two charts: a standard chart for hourly staff and a separate chart for Leadership / salaried employees. Set each person's type in their record. In both, PTO is issued as a lump sum at the start of each year (Jan 1) based on tenure, on top of any rolled-over balance, capped at the accrual cap; and on each hire-date anniversary the employee receives only the increase over the prior year — not the full total — so several anniversaries add nothing.

Standard (hourly) chart

New hires get 3 days upon hire (usable after 90 days).

Sick Leave

Frontloaded 40 hours (5 days) each benefit year for employees with 30+ days of service. Usable beginning on the 90th day of employment — the same 90-day gate as the first PTO. Because it's frontloaded, unused sick leave does not carry over to the next benefit year.

How this tracker works: you enter only name, dates, and PTO/sick hours used. New hires get the base hire grant for their chart (3 days standard, 10 days leadership). Then there are two automatic grants: on January 1 the full annual lump sum for the employee's tenure is frontloaded on top of whatever rolled over (capped at the accrual cap, and PTO used resets to zero); and on each hire-date anniversary only the increase over the prior year is added (so years 2, 4, 6, 8 — and 16+ — add nothing). Sick leave is frontloaded each year and resets Dec 31 by default, since unused sick doesn't carry over. The starting bank for a new employee is estimated from tenure assuming no prior shortfall — use Advanced → adjust starting PTO bank if your records differ. Updates apply whenever you open the page. Hours use 8 hours = 1 day.

Eligibility rules & reference

Medical / Health insurance

Eligible the 1st of the month following 60 days of employment. Example: hired Jan 8 → 60 days lands in early March → eligible April 1. New hires should receive enrollment information ahead of that date. The app computes this date for you the moment a start date is entered.

401(k) & Profit Sharing

Per the Plan's Summary Plan Description, an employee is eligible at age 18 after one year of service with 1,000 hours, entering on the first day of the month. This tracker uses the 1-year hire anniversary as the eligibility date plus a 1,000-hour checkbox you confirm (since hours can't be derived from a date alone). A 401(k) benefit notice should go out about one month before the anniversary.

Rehires: employees who already met eligibility re-enter after one hour of service. Set their medical or 401(k) date to N/A or a custom date in the record as needed — the rest stays automatic.
